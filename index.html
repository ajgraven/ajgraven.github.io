About

I'm a fifth year math PhD student at Caltech working with Nikolai Makarov. My research interests lie primarily within in geometric function theory, potential theory, complex analysis, and complex dynamics. I graduated from Cornell University in 2021 with a BA in Mathematics. See my CV for more information.

Gallery

Complex Dynamics Visualization Tool

GPU-accelerated interactive explorer for complex dynamical systems.

More

Visualize Mandelbrot sets, Julia sets, Burning Ship fractals, and other parameter spaces in real time. Supports custom functions f(z,c), adjustable escape conditions, zoom, and PNG export. Built with Cindy.js and CindyGL (WebGL).

Conformal Map Visualizer

Interactive visualization of how complex maps deform the unit disk.

More

Draws the image of a polar grid on the unit disk (or its exterior) under a user-defined complex function f(z). Grid lines are colored by arg(f′(z)), revealing critical points and conformal structure. Supports draggable complex parameters, real parameter sliders, and a library of preset maps.

Domain Coloring

GPU-accelerated domain coloring of complex functions.

More

Colors each pixel z by f(z): hue encodes argument, brightness encodes log-magnitude. Zeros appear as black points where all colors converge; poles appear as bright spots where colors cycle rapidly. Supports custom functions, pan/zoom, 12 presets, and PNG export.

Logarithmic Potential Explorer

Interactive GPU visualization of logarithmic potentials and their harmonic conjugates.

More

Place positive charges (zeros) and negative charges (poles) in the plane and watch the logarithmic potential update in real time. Hue encodes the harmonic conjugate (stream function); brightness level curves are equipotentials. Built with Cindy.js and CindyGL.

Potential Theory Sandbox

Interactive tool for exploring harmonic functions and equilibrium measures.

More

Draws equipotential lines and gradient flows for user-defined charge configurations in the plane. Demonstrates connections between logarithmic potential theory and electrostatics, including balayage and Green's functions.

Orbital Coverage Simulator

Ergodic-theory approach to satellite ground-station coverage statistics.

More

Computes long-term view periods between satellites and ground stations for elliptical orbits under J2 perturbations, using ergodic theory to replace expensive Monte Carlo simulations. Related to work presented at AAS 2021.
